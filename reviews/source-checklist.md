# Source Checklist

Use this checklist when source fidelity matters: research extraction, summaries, source-led copy, transcript work, competitor reading, and proof claims.

- The output preserves the source's meaning before adding interpretation.
- Direct quotes or near-quotes are clearly distinguished from paraphrase.
- The review identifies which lines are captured source language, light edits, synthesis, or unproven.
- No polished public line is built from blended sources without being marked as synthesis.
- The source's market words, workday situations, proof mechanisms, and objections are captured before recommendations.
- Claims are traceable to a source, a concrete work object, or a real workday situation.
- The output names what Vastpakt can borrow and what it must not copy.
- Uncertainty stays visible where the source is ambiguous, incomplete, or second-hand.
