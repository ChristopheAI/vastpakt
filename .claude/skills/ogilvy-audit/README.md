# Ogilvy Audit

A Claude skill that audits your writing against David Ogilvy's 10 rules and the Roman & Raphaelson principles from *Writing That Works*.

## What it does

Paste a draft (memo, email, ad copy, article, landing page, anything). Ask Claude to audit it. You'll get back:

- A one-line overall read
- Violations tagged 🔴 Critical / 🟡 Moderate / 🟢 Minor
- Each violation quotes the exact text and proposes a specific fix
- A "what's working" section so you know what to keep doing
- A reminder of the rules that can't be audited from text alone (sleep on it, get a colleague to read it, check quotations, etc.)

## What it doesn't do

- It doesn't rewrite your draft. The audit is the value. Rewriting without a diagnosis just swaps one voice for another.
- It doesn't audit fiction, poetry, or literary prose. Ogilvy's rules are for business writing and persuasion.
- It doesn't pad. If the writing is clean, the audit is short.

## The rules it checks

**From Ogilvy's 1982 "How to Write" memo:**
- Natural voice (write the way you talk)
- Short words, short sentences, short paragraphs
- No jargon
- Quotations flagged for verification
- Read-aloud test
- Clear call to action

**From Roman & Raphaelson's *Writing That Works*:**
- Comprehension (can the reader understand what you're asking?)
- Completeness (did you actually say the thing?)
- Honesty (no hedging, no euphemisms, straight talk on bad news)
- Facts with context (no "unstrung gems")

## How to use it

Install the skill, then just ask Claude to audit your writing:

- "Audit this memo against Ogilvy's rules"
- "Review this email draft"
- "Is this landing page copy well-written?"
- "Score this for clarity"

Or just paste a draft and ask "what's wrong with this?" — the skill will trigger.
