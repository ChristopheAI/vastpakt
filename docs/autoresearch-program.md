# Vastpakt Autoresearch Program

Date: 2026-05-27

## Purpose

Use Exa and Swipefile as a training field for judgment. Each homepage round should make one concrete KMO workday scene more recognizable, more inspectable, and less like marketing theatre.

## Core Question

How can I treat every homepage round as a small experiment: sharpen one scene, view it in the browser, judge why it does or does not work, and only keep what makes the site less marketing theatre and more recognizable KMO workday?

## Current Critical Loop

Offer certainty now outranks copy polish.

The homepage may stay small, but it cannot stay vague. Competitor review showed that adjacent players give the buyer a clearer commercial shape:

- Axxit: Excel and loose tools to one working system.
- Flowlift: call, time wasters, plan, prices, quick win and KPI.
- Codelines: scan, analysis, report and action plan.

Vastpakt should not copy those larger shapes, but it must be equally clear about the first step. `Stuur één voorbeeld` is only strong if the visitor knows exactly what happens next.

Autoresearch should now grill every homepage loop against this question:

```text
Is the first response a concrete offer moment, or only a friendly filter?
```

Do not solve this with invented copy. Only publish it when the operating truth is known:

- what Christophe sends back within 24 hours;
- whether the first response is free, paid, or only a fit check;
- whether the response is written, a call proposal, a mini diagnosis, or something else;
- where the first contact ends and paid/active Vastpakt work begins.

Loop 50 tightened this further: the missing piece is probably not a call, a scan label, or more person-trust copy. It is a small named first-response artifact. Adjacent offers make the first step tangible by promising a PDF, written report, map, priority list, score, direct no, or recommended next step. Vastpakt's likely equivalent is a short written first look at the submitted example, not a broad audit.

## Loop

1. Pick one scene, section, CTA, or proof object.
2. Read 2-5 external sources through Exa, including Swipefile when relevant.
3. Extract the working mechanism:
   - what works;
   - what Vastpakt can borrow;
   - what Vastpakt must not copy;
   - why the mechanism works.
4. Change one bounded part of `index.html` or `styles.css`.
5. Open `http://localhost:5502/` and inspect the result.
6. Keep or discard with a `want` verdict:
   - Keep, want it makes the work object clearer.
   - Discard, want it sounds clever but does not show work.
   - Revise, want it creates recognition but not yet enough trust.
7. Log the result in `docs/experiments/homepage-experiments.tsv`.

## Editable Surface

- Primary: `index.html`
- Secondary: `styles.css`
- Notes: `docs/copy/` and `docs/experiments/`

## Constraints

- Do not publish internal words: research, Swipefile, proof mechanism, agent, copy move, ordinary sentence, source-backed.
- Do not copy the visual style of Swipefile or any competitor.
- Do not add broad claims, fake metrics, ROI claims, or AI-agency language.
- Do not expand multiple sections in one experiment unless a single scene requires it.

## Evaluation Criteria

An experiment is worth keeping when:

- a KMO reader can recognize the workday situation faster;
- the page shows a concrete object, status, owner, proof, next step, or missing detail;
- the claim is closer to the proof object than before;
- the line can be explained with `want`;
- the page becomes calmer or sharper, not just longer.

An experiment should be discarded when:

- it makes Vastpakt sound like generic process consulting;
- it explains the method before showing the work;
- it adds a clever line without a visible work object;
- it copies an external style instead of translating the mechanism;
- it creates more page weight without more trust.
- it lowers friction but leaves the first offer moment vague.
- it says someone will reply, but does not name the useful artifact or decision the reader receives.
