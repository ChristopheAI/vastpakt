## Exa Pattern

Strong adjacent offers make the first response tangible. They do not only say "we reply" or "we check fit".

Repeated patterns:

- short intake, then a named output;
- clear timing;
- written artifact or direct answer;
- fit/no-fit boundary;
- optional next step;
- the deliverable is useful even without buying the larger service.

Examples:

- Wolrix: submit stack and challenge, receive a 3-page PDF in 24 hours with current-stack read, top three problems, and recommended next step.
- Golden Horizons: structured intake, plain-English audit, where AI is worth trying, what to leave alone, and what to do first.
- LittleGiant: short call, custom audit report within 48 hours, findings and clear recommendation.
- Admentus: intake reviewed personally, custom strategy snapshot with a short video, bottleneck diagnosis, 2-3 opportunities, and next move.
- Sterling North: describe problem, personal review, direct response within 2 business days: fit or not, why, and what approach might fit instead.
- Applied Leverage: intake and diagnostic produce a one-page priority map with top three automation moves.
- Marshland: fixed-fee audit produces a written workflow map, recommendations, and what is ruled out.

## Swipefile Discipline

Local Swipefile notes already pointed to this:

- first contact should be a tiny diagnostic exchange, not a heavy lead form;
- first scan should be small enough to test;
- output should be easy to summarize in four lines;
- show the object instead of explaining the method;
- every section should reduce doubt.

## Strict Verdict

The answer is not "add more Christophe".

The answer is: make the 24-hour response a small artifact.

Vastpakt's first response should probably not be a call, report, roadmap, scan, or automation proposal. Those drift toward competitors. The source-backed pattern suggests a smaller written artifact that makes one submitted work situation inspectable.

## Candidate Vastpakt Shape

Only if operationally true, the first response can be:

```text
Binnen 24 uur krijg je een kort eerste zicht op je voorbeeld:
- past dit wel of niet bij Vastpakt;
- waar het werk vandaag lijkt los te komen;
- welke ene vraag eerst zichtbaar moet worden;
- wat de kleinste volgende stap is.
```

This should be treated as a first-response artifact, not a full Eerste Zicht deliverable.

## Classification

- `kort eerste zicht op je voorbeeld`: Vastpakt translation / candidate artifact label.
- `past dit wel of niet`: user-provided fit/no-fit direction, already public-safe.
- `waar het werk vandaag lijkt los te komen`: cautious diagnosis language; needs care because it is based only on the submitted example.
- `welke ene vraag eerst zichtbaar moet worden`: existing offer/contact language.
- `kleinste volgende stap`: existing offer-contact direction, but must not become a hidden proposal.

## What Vastpakt Must Not Copy

- no large PDF promise unless real;
- no score, maturity model, KPI, savings estimate, or roadmap;
- no "AI audit" framing;
- no sales-call disguised as diagnostic;
- no broad promise that one example reveals the whole company.

## Remaining Operating Truth Needed

Before public copy changes:

- Can Christophe actually send this in writing within 24 hours?
- Is it free?
- Is it by email?
- Does it include a call link only if useful?
- Does "kleinste volgende stap" mean a suggestion, a paid Eerste Zicht, or only a next question?

## Recommended Next Homepage Move

If the above is true, change the person/contact area from generic reply assurance to a named first-response artifact. Keep it small and written.

If it is not true, weaken the 24-hour response to a fit/no-fit answer only.
