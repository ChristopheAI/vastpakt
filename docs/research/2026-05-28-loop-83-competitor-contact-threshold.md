# Loop 83 - Competitor Contact Threshold

## Question

After moving contact directly after `Eerste Zicht`, should the contact block stay as-is or be made lighter using competitor and swipefile discipline?

## Verdict

Make it lighter.

The competitor lesson is not to copy their forms or promises. The useful pattern is that the first action is clear: tell us the issue, book the intake, request a quote, send the slowing-down point, or ask the question.

For Vastpakt, the first action should stay smaller than that: send one concrete workday example.

## Exa Sources Reviewed

- Codelines: starts with a free analysis/scan frame and uses a sentence-style contact form.
- Zendev: asks the visitor to pitch an issue, then promises an approach/proposal.
- Smet Software Solutions: asks for a situation plus budget range and urgency.
- Karel Van Landschoot: asks the visitor to briefly tell the question or project and promises a clear answer with a next step.
- Onoweb: uses a 30-minute kennismaking and a proposal process.
- UKIYO: asks what part is slowing the team down and promises a clear automation plan and next steps.
- Knops: uses a 30-minute introduction, 24-hour response and social proof.

## Pattern

Competitors reduce doubt by naming the first action and what follows, but often raise the perceived size of the step with:

- intake;
- analysis;
- audit or scan;
- proposal;
- plan;
- budget range;
- project framing;
- social proof and scarcity.

## Vastpakt Translation

Borrow:

- clear first action;
- concrete next response;
- direct person/trust near the form.

Do not borrow:

- analysis/scan/audit language;
- budget range or project request;
- popularity claims;
- fake urgency;
- broad automation or process optimization framing.

## Change

Contact heading changed from:

`Stuur één voorbeeld waar je tijd verliest door zoeken, overtypen of rondvragen.`

to:

`Stuur één voorbeeld uit je werkdag.`

The specific filters stay in the contact exchange below the heading.

## Boundary

This does not create a new proof claim or promise. It only reduces the effort implied by the final heading.
