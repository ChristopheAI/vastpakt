# Loop 69 - Hero Internal Object Label

## Question

What is the strictest remaining homepage weakness after the LinkedIn detour?

## Sources checked

- Rendered homepage at `http://localhost:5502/?loop=68#top`.
- Local copy classification notes for `Situatiekaart` and `Eerste Zicht`.
- Exa scan of Belgian/KMO automation and maatwerk pages around manual work, Excel, CRM, audits, scans, roadmaps and first-step offers.

## Source signal

Adjacent pages lean heavily on recognizable market objects and promises:

- manual work, Excel, CRM, e-mail, double entry, one system, dashboards, audit, scan, roadmap, quick wins, time savings;
- competitors often make the first step concrete through a scan, audit, first conversation, report or roadmap;
- this creates a risk for Vastpakt: if the homepage uses an internal object name too early, it can feel like another invented method word instead of a normal workday example.

## Verdict

The strongest immediate weakness is not the hero headline or the examples. It is the first-viewport label `Situatiekaart van één offerte`.

`Situatiekaart` is useful internally, but the rules already mark it as unproven market language. Above the fold, the visitor should first recognize the ordinary object: one sent offer and the missing follow-up.

## Change

Replace the first-viewport public label with:

`Voorbeeld van één offerte`

Keep the proof object itself unchanged.

## Boundary

Do not replace this with scan, audit, roadmap, report, ROI, savings, or automation language. The point is to reduce internal language, not to copy competitor category language.
