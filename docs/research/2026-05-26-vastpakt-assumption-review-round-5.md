# Vastpakt Assumption Review - Round 5

Date: 2026-05-26

Purpose: decide what survives after the open Flemish/Belgian Reddit reading, before writing homepage copy.

Verdict:

```text
The strategic direction survives.
The public wording must broaden.
```

Vastpakt should still start with `eerst zicht`, but the public promise should not be reduced to `handwerk`, `workflow`, or `automatisering`. The open reading showed a larger Flemish pain: people want to know where they stand before they decide, pay, follow up, dispute, delegate, automate, or continue.

## Assumption Review

### Assumption 1: "Handwerk" Is The Core Problem

Status: partially rejected.

What survived:

- Hidden manual work is still a useful internal detector.
- Copying, retyping, waiting, routing, and reporting remain good discovery signals.

What changed:

- `Handwerk` is too narrow and too operational as public language.
- People do not only complain about tasks. They complain about uncertainty, proof, fallback, legality, timing, and not knowing where they stand.

Decision:

```text
Use "handwerk" internally.
Do not make it the public headline.
```

Better public language:

```text
Eerst zien waar ge staat.
```

### Assumption 2: "Eerst Een Werking Zichtbaar Maken" Is Still The Anchor

Status: confirmed, but broadened.

What survived:

- The sequence is still right: first visibility, then change.
- The promise still needs restraint: not everything, not immediately automation, not a huge project.

What changed:

- `werking` should hold more than process flow. It should also hold status, proof, decision context, and fallback.

Decision:

```text
Keep "Eerst een werking zichtbaar maken. Dan pas kiezen wat moet veranderen."
Add "Eerst zien waar ge staat" as the warmer public-facing expression.
```

### Assumption 3: The Main Reader Is A Flemish KMO Operator

Status: confirmed.

What survived:

- Flemish/Belgian readers respond to concrete, practical cases.
- The strongest language comes from small businesses, contractors, freelancers, people in bijberoep, and customers dealing with local service providers.

What changed:

- The reader is not always the one doing the messy work. Sometimes the reader is the person waiting, disputing, paying, or trying to understand what happened.

Decision:

```text
Keep KMO/zaakvoerder/teamlead as primary.
Allow customer-facing proof scenarios where they reveal what a KMO must make visible.
```

### Assumption 4: Proof Objects Should Lead

Status: confirmed with a warning.

What survived:

- Proof objects remain stronger than claims.
- Situatiekaart remains useful as the first visible-work object.

What changed:

- Proof objects can feel bureaucratic if framed as extra documentation.
- The proof object must reduce anxiety and ambiguity, not add paperwork.

Decision:

```text
Proof object yes.
Paperwork feeling no.
```

Required copy guardrail:

```text
Meer zicht, niet meer papierwerk.
```

### Assumption 5: The Homepage Should Focus On Follow-Up

Status: partially confirmed.

What survived:

- Follow-up remains a highly recognizable problem, especially with offers, requests, invoices, and appointments.

What changed:

- The larger job is status clarity: where does this stand, who is aan zet, what is proven, what can be decided now?

Decision:

```text
Follow-up is one proof family.
Status clarity is the larger frame.
```

### Assumption 6: Excel, WhatsApp, Mail And The Bookkeeper Are The Problem

Status: rejected.

What survived:

- These are common places where work fragments.

What changed:

- The tools are often good enough at low volume.
- The problem is not the tool, but the route between tools, people, decisions, and proof.

Decision:

```text
Do not attack Excel, WhatsApp, mail, or the boekhouder.
Show where the route between them becomes unclear.
```

### Assumption 7: Automation Belongs Out Of The First Promise

Status: confirmed with nuance.

What survived:

- Automation should not lead.
- Tool choice should not lead.

What changed:

- Automation can belong later when the visible rule is obvious enough.
- People accept software when it catches what no human should have to keep noticing manually.

Decision:

```text
Visibility first.
Automation only after the obvious check, rule, or route is clear.
```

## Public-Facing Language Test

### Best Core Line

```text
Eerst zien waar ge staat.
Daarna pas kiezen wat moet veranderen.
```

Why:

- Warmer and more local than `werking zichtbaar maken`.
- Broad enough for offers, invoices, planning, proof, decisions, and owner dependency.
- Less bureaucratic than `workflow`.
- Less tool-like than `handwerk`.

Risk:

- Could become vague if not immediately followed by concrete scenarios.

Required support line:

```text
Vastpakt toont waar werk vandaag blijft hangen tussen aanvraag, afspraak, offerte, factuur en opvolging.
```

### Keep As Method Line

```text
Eerst een werking zichtbaar maken.
Dan pas kiezen wat moet veranderen.
```

Why:

- Still precise as method.
- Works well below the hero or in the method section.

### Keep As Reassurance Line

```text
Meer zicht, niet meer papierwerk.
```

Why:

- Directly answers the fear that Vastpakt creates extra administration.
- Strong Flemish/KMO restraint.

### Avoid As Public Headline

```text
Waar zit het handwerk verstopt?
```

Why:

- Useful internal diagnostic question.
- Too abstract and consultant-like for first contact.

## Proof Examples That Still Feel Real

### Tier 1: Homepage-Ready

1. Offerte Waarna Het Stil Wordt
2. Offerteaanvraag Zonder Antwoord
3. Zaakvoerder Als Opvolging
4. Factuur Zonder Seintje

Why:

- Concrete.
- Flemish/Belgian enough.
- Status and next step are naturally visible.
- They do not require explaining software.

### Tier 2: Strong Support

1. Excel-Factuur Zonder Route
2. Kantoorwerk Dat Het Werk Tegenhoudt
3. Detailfactuur Zonder Bewijs
4. Boekhouder-Handoff Zonder Overzicht

Why:

- Useful, but some are more administrative or conflict-heavy.
- Better for lower page sections, examples library, or FAQ.

### Use Carefully

1. WhatsApp-bestelling Zonder Spoor
2. Rapport Zonder Beslissing
3. CRM/Lead Routing examples

Why:

- Good patterns, but either less Flemish from current sources or too tool/sector-specific.

## What The Homepage Should Not Do Yet

- Do not lead with AI.
- Do not lead with automation.
- Do not lead with workflow.
- Do not attack Excel.
- Do not make the scan feel like an administrative audit.
- Do not make proofcards sound like portfolio case studies before real examples exist.

## What The Homepage Should Do Next

1. Lead with status clarity:

```text
Eerst zien waar ge staat.
Daarna pas kiezen wat moet veranderen.
```

2. Immediately ground it:

```text
Offertes die stilvallen. Facturen die pas achteraf duidelijk worden. Aanvragen waar niemand zeker van weet wie aan zet is.
```

3. Reassure:

```text
Niet alles herwerken. Niet meteen automatiseren. Eerst één stuk zichtbaar maken.
```

4. Show three concrete proof situations:

- Offerte Waarna Het Stil Wordt
- Factuur Zonder Seintje
- Zaakvoerder Als Opvolging

5. Make the first action a situation, not a meeting:

```text
Toon één situatie waar ge vandaag niet zeker weet waar het staat.
```

## Decision For The Superpowers Spec

Update the main spec with these corrections:

- Public hero may use `Eerst zien waar ge staat`.
- `Handwerk` stays as a diagnostic lens, not headline language.
- Add guardrail: `Meer zicht, niet meer papierwerk`.
- Broaden `opvolging hangt los` into status clarity: owner, next step, proof, decision, fallback.
- Keep `Eerst een werking zichtbaar maken` as the method anchor.
