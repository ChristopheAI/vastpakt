# Loop 126 - Align the Three First-Response Touchpoints

Date: 2026-05-29

## Decision Intent

The first response now has a confirmed operating truth (DESIGN.md section 8: a free, short written first look, four parts). But it appears on the page in three places that overlapped and drifted:

1. Exchange card "Wij sturen terug" (Eerste Zicht).
2. The right-column deliverables list next to it ("Wat wordt duidelijk").
3. The person-section fact "Binnen 24 uur".

Make all three echo one canonical promise, pulled from section 8, without adding weight.

## Scene

The Eerste Zicht offer block. The exchange card and the deliverables list sit side by side and said nearly the same thing (both listed where it hangs / which question / fit). Pure duplication at the exact decision point.

## Evidence Lenses (Exa)

Mechanism: state one promise once, echo it across sections with a different angle, never repeat the same claim.

- **Searchlab** (`https://searchlab.nl/en/guides/small-business-messaging-framework`): "every piece of copy pulls from the same source instead of being invented from scratch... place the framework next to the writing surface." Also: rewrite the slot from "what we do" to "what you get". High utility: DESIGN.md section 8 is that source; the right column should be "what you get".
- **Sense Central** (`https://sensecentral.com/messaging-framework-value-proposition-benefits-objections-and-proof/`): "Every touchpoint should make the same promise in a slightly different way, so visitors never feel they entered a different business. Repetition creates memory. Consistency creates trust."
- **Browsely** (`https://browsely.ai/blog/audit-landing-pages-for-message-consistency`): keep lines that match the core message, rewrite inconsistent ones, remove duplicate or conflicting claims.
- **Verlua** (`https://www.verlua.com/blog/service-page-copywriting-guide`): a "What's Included" list "tells them exactly what they are buying"; one primary CTA with consistent wording.

## Mechanism

- **What works:** one canonical promise, echoed with distinct angles; a "what you get" list that names the exact contents.
- **What Vastpakt borrows:** make the right column the four-part shape of the artifact (the contents), keep the exchange card as the one-line deal, let the person fact echo it in the person register.
- **What Vastpakt must not copy:** restating the same three bullets in two adjacent blocks; metrics or "what we do" framing.
- **Why it works:** at the decision point the reader sees the deal and exactly what lands in the inbox, stated once each. Consistency builds trust velocity.

## Change

Three aligned edits, all sourced from DESIGN.md section 8:

1. Exchange card "Wij sturen terug" shortened to the named deal: "Een korte geschreven eerste blik op dat ene voorbeeld, binnen 24 uur." (Dropped the inline contents and honest no; they now live where they belong.)
2. Right column "Wat wordt duidelijk" became "Wat je terugkrijgt", the four-part shape: wat je stuurde in jouw woorden / waar status, bewijs of volgende stap onduidelijk wordt / de kleine volgende stap, als je verder wil / wat dit niet is, eerlijk gezegd. This adds the missing part 1 (the "you were heard" beat).
3. Person fact "Binnen 24 uur" sub changed to "Christophe schrijft zelf je eerste blik" so it echoes the named artifact.

## Want Verdict

Keep. The three touchpoints now read as one promise from three angles: the deal (card), the contents (list), the person (fact). No duplicate claim, the honest no lives in the list and the person register, part 1 is now present. Sharper, not heavier: the card got shorter, the list became the meaningful structure. Browser-verified: the offer block reads cleanly, no overflow, no duplication.
