# Flowlift outcome correction

Date: 2026-05-31

Status: correction layer on top of older Flowlift/Vastpakt notes. This is internal research, not public copy.

## Why this note exists

Christophe corrected the earlier framing:

```text
Vastpakt moet daar niet voor zitten.
De outcome is hetzelfde.
```

Verdict: correct.

The older internal line `Vastpakt must stay one step earlier` is too weak and can mislead the redesign. It makes Vastpakt sound like a pre-step before Flowlift, Axxit or another automation/software provider. That is not the desired positioning.

## What Flowlift shows live

Observed source: `https://www.flowlift.be/`

- Flowlift frames the homepage around routine processes, existing tools, hours won back, a clear plan, quick wins, KPI's, pricing and implementation.
- The visible use-case categories are operational: support/email, sales/quotes, invoices/reporting, customer service, inventory, Excel to CRM, Outlook/Teams follow-up, CRM to Exact.

Observed source: `https://www.flowlift.be/use-case-excel-naar-crm-sync.html`

- Opening outcome: stop double administrative work.
- Work object: Excel and CRM.
- Before-state: daily copying, copy-paste work, duplicate input, errors, CRM behind on Excel.
- Route: automatic synchronisation.
- Result: less manual copying, accurate data, current data in both systems, more time for customer contact.

Observed source: `https://www.flowlift.be/use-case-offerte-automatisering-bouw.html`

- Opening outcome: faster quotes and higher conversion.
- Work object: quote, measurement sheet, prices, margin, follow-up, Excel templates.
- Before-state: 2 hours per quote, slow turnaround, inconsistent prices, no follow-up, Excel chaos.
- Route: quote generator and automatic follow-up.
- Result: more quotes, faster quotes, better follow-up and higher conversion.

Observed source: `https://www.flowlift.be/use-case-email-automatisering-kmo.html`

- Opening outcome: from email chaos to control.
- Work object: incoming mails, price requests, order confirmations, delivery questions, technical questions, invoicing and complaints.
- Before-state: hundreds of emails, manual processing, status checking and forwarding.
- Route: email classification, automatic answers and integrations.
- Result: less response time, fewer errors and more control.

## Corrected interpretation

The outcome is not:

```text
Eerst zichtbaar maken.
```

That is only Vastpakt's route.

The buyer outcome is closer to:

```text
Minder dubbel werk.
Duidelijkere opvolging.
Minder handmatig controleren.
Status, bewijs en volgende actie die niet meer los zitten.
Werk dat slimmer, duidelijker of automatisch kan verderlopen.
```

That is why the CopyJay-inspired line is directionally right:

```text
Ik bouw de brug tussen je dubbel werk vandaag
en wat straks slimmer, duidelijker of automatisch kan.
```

Classification: user-approved Vastpakt translation, still not final public homepage copy.

## Outcome priority decision

Christophe confirmed the direction after the Flowlift correction.

Internal decision for the next wireframe:

```text
Primary outcome: minder dubbel werk.
Secondary outcome: duidelijkere opvolging.
Bridge outcome: wat straks slimmer, duidelijker of automatisch kan.
```

This does not approve a final public H1 yet. It sets the hierarchy for the redesign:

1. Lead with the wanted end-state.
2. Show the work object that makes it recognizable.
3. Explain Christophe's route only after the outcome is clear.

## What changes for the redesign

Wrong redesign frame:

```text
Vastpakt zit voor automatisering.
Vastpakt maakt zichtbaar, daarna komt eventueel iemand anders.
```

Correct redesign frame:

```text
Vastpakt werkt naar dezelfde gewenste eindtoestand:
minder dubbel werk, duidelijkere opvolging, minder handwerk, meer controle.

De route is anders:
Christophe vertrekt bij één concrete werksituatie en maakt zichtbaar waar status, bewijs, eigenaar, afspraak of volgende actie loskomt.
```

## What Vastpakt may borrow

- Use-case thinking by work object: Excel, CRM, mail, offerte, factuur, opvolging.
- Before-state close to the workday: copy-paste, dubbele invoer, vergeten opvolging, handmatig controleren, data op verschillende plekken.
- Outcome language that is concrete enough to want: minder dubbel werk, duidelijkere opvolging, minder fouten, actuele info, sneller reageren.
- Case structure: before-state, route, result, next action.

## What Vastpakt must not copy

- Measured time-savings, ROI, revenue or conversion claims without its own proof.
- Automation-first hero.
- KPI, roadmap, pricing and implementation language as first-frame promise.
- AI/tool/integration stack as proof.
- Case-study certainty where Vastpakt only has an exploratory or early proof object.

## Redesign implication

The homepage must not hide behind `zichtbaar maken`.

Use `zichtbaar maken` internally as the mechanism:

```text
Where is the work currently loose?
```

Use the public first layer around the outcome:

```text
Dubbel werk vandaag.
Straks slimmer, duidelijker of automatisch.
```

Open proof requirement:

- Which concrete Vastpakt cases can already prove less double work, clearer follow-up, less manual checking, or a built working route?
- Which outcomes are only possible future outcomes and must stay unclaimed?
- The first homepage outcome is now decided internally: `minder dubbel werk`, supported by `duidelijkere opvolging`.
