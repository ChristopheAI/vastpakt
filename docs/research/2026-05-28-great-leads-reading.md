# Great Leads - Vastpakt reading notes

Date: 2026-05-28
Source: user-provided PDF, `Great leads.pdf`
Use: internal lead and homepage-opening discipline for Vastpakt. Not public copy.

## What this source adds

This source is about choosing the right opening based on customer awareness.

Core lesson:

The lead is not chosen by taste. It is chosen by what the visitor already knows:

- about the product;
- about the problem;
- about possible solutions;
- about their own desire or worry.

## Rule of One

The source reinforces one big idea:

- one good idea;
- one core emotion;
- one story or compelling fact;
- one desirable benefit;
- one inevitable response.

Vastpakt implication:

- One reader: Flemish KMO operator with manual work and unclear follow-up.
- One problem: work looks like it runs, until someone asks where it stands.
- One benefit: you see what must remain visible before anything changes.
- One response: send one example.

## Awareness

The source's most important question:

`What does your customer already know?`

Vastpakt implication:

The homepage visitor is probably not product-aware.

They likely do not know:

- what `Eerste Zicht` means;
- that Vastpakt has a method;
- that one example is enough;
- that visibility before change is the offer.

They are more likely:

- problem-aware: they recognize searching, retyping, asking around, unclear follow-up;
- possibly solution-aware: they know tools, automation, CRM, Excel cleanup or software could help, but are unsure where to start.

Therefore the homepage should not open with an offer lead or product lead.

## Lead type decision for Vastpakt

### Offer Lead

Not right for the hero.

Reason:

- `Eerste Zicht` is not yet known enough.
- A direct offer lead would require the visitor to already understand and value the offer.

Offer-style clarity still belongs near CTA/contact:

- what to send;
- what comes back;
- who replies;
- when.

### Promise Lead

Risky as the main hero.

Reason:

- Big promises would sound like generic automation, consultancy or AI claims.
- Vastpakt proof is still practice-case based, not broad market proof.

Useful only if the promise stays sober:

- see what is open;
- see what is missing;
- know what first needs to become visible.

### Problem-Solution Lead

Best current fit for Vastpakt.

Reason:

- The visitor recognizes the problem before they know the offer.
- The current hero already works this way:
  `Alles loopt. Tot iemand vraagt waar het staat.`

This is closer to the real awareness level than:

- `Eerste Zicht voor jouw KMO`;
- `Automatiseer je processen`;
- `Wij bouwen slimme tools`.

### Secret Lead

Dangerous for Vastpakt.

Reason:

- It encourages invented terms and hidden mechanisms.
- Vastpakt should avoid making `situatiekaart`, `tijdlek`, or named mechanisms carry the public claim.

### Proclamation Lead

Mostly too loud for Vastpakt.

Could be useful internally to find bold observations, but not as public tone unless a real, specific fact supports it.

### Story Lead

Useful for practice cases.

Reason:

- The examples grid should let the visitor experience the work situation.
- The story must stay short and connected to the CTA.

## Practical homepage grill

Before changing a hero, section intro, or CTA, ask:

1. What awareness level is this line written for?
2. Does the visitor already know the offer, or only the problem?
3. Is the line too direct for someone who does not yet trust the category?
4. Is there one big idea, or several?
5. What one emotion is created: relief, recognition, safety, urgency, worry?
6. What one action follows naturally?
7. Is proof close enough to carry the promise?

## Current implication for Vastpakt

The current hero direction is correct:

`Alles loopt. Tot iemand vraagt waar het staat.`

This is a problem-solution lead built around recognition, not an offer/product lead.

The next homepage risk is not the hero. It is consistency:

- keep `Eerste Zicht` explained after recognition;
- keep CTA/contact direct and offer-like;
- keep examples story-like and concrete;
- avoid secret/proclamation style names and claims.

## What Vastpakt should not import

- Hype around big promises.
- Secret-style invented names.
- Proclamation tone without source proof.
- Offer-first hero copy around an offer name the market does not yet know.
- Multiple benefits in one section.
- Clever leads that delay the workday problem too long.

## Fresh-eyes reread

The sharper lesson is not simply "use a problem-solution lead".

The sharper lesson is:

Different parts of the same homepage can speak to different awareness levels, but they must not get mixed up.

### Current Vastpakt awareness map

Hero:

- Visitor state: problem-aware.
- Job: make the visitor recognize the workday problem.
- Correct lead style: problem-solution.
- Current direction is right: `Alles loopt. Tot iemand vraagt waar het staat.`

Examples:

- Visitor state: problem-aware moving toward solution-aware.
- Job: show that Vastpakt is not talking abstractly; real work objects have been handled.
- Correct lead style: short story/proof.
- Risk: the cases can become a list of capabilities instead of one golden thread toward `send one example`.

Eerste Zicht:

- Visitor state: solution-aware.
- Job: explain the small first offer after recognition and proof.
- Correct lead style: clear offer explanation, but not a hero-level offer lead.
- Risk: the offer name still needs plain explanation every time it matters.

Person/contact:

- Visitor state: closer to product-aware.
- Job: remove action doubt.
- Correct lead style: direct offer/instruction.
- Risk: any diagnostic phrasing can raise the threshold again.

### Stronger strategic conclusion

Do not force the whole homepage into one lead type.

Instead:

- hero = problem-solution;
- examples = story/proof;
- offer section = small solution explanation;
- contact = direct offer/instruction.

This is why pushing `Eerste Zicht` higher would likely weaken the page. The visitor is not ready for the offer name until the problem and proof have done their job.

### New grill question

For every section, ask:

`Is this section speaking to the awareness level the visitor likely has at this exact point in the page?`

If not, the section may be saying something true at the wrong moment.
