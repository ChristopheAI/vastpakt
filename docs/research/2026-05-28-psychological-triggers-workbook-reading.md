## What this source adds

This workbook is a checklist of persuasion triggers. For Vastpakt, the useful lesson is not to stack triggers. It is to choose only the triggers that make the first action clearer, more believable and less risky.

The strongest safe triggers for Vastpakt are:

- involvement and ownership;
- objection raising and resolution;
- integrity;
- credibility;
- simplicity;
- specificity;
- linking;
- harmonizing with the prospect;
- honesty.

## Useful triggers for Vastpakt

### Consistency

Useful only in a light way.

Vastpakt implication:

- A checked signal, viewed example or submitted form should all point to the same next action: one example.
- Do not create a ladder of commitments or upsells on the homepage.

### Product nature

Vastpakt must understand its real nature:

- not a generic automation service;
- not an AI agency;
- not a process optimization consultancy;
- not a report factory;
- first, one concrete work situation made visible.

### Prospect nature

The prospect is likely not looking for "Vastpakt" by name.

They recognize:

- searching;
- overtyping;
- asking around;
- unclear follow-up;
- work stuck in mail, Excel, CRM, Word, Teams or one person's head.

### Objection raising

Useful and important.

Likely objections:

- Is this going to become a big project?
- Do we need another tool?
- Is this automation?
- Do I need to prepare a full briefing?
- Will this be too vague?
- Will I lose control?

Vastpakt should raise and resolve only objections that are already likely in the visitor's mind.

### Objection resolution

Vastpakt already resolves some objections:

- one example is enough;
- Christophe replies himself;
- within 24 hours;
- honest yes/no if it fits;
- no tool change as first step;
- control remains with the person doing the work.

Keep this plain. Do not invent anti-sales slogans.

### Involvement and ownership

This is very relevant.

The visitor should mentally place their own mail, offerte, Excel row, CRM note, Word document or afspraak into the page.

The form label `Welk voorbeeld wil je tonen?` supports this better than asking the visitor to diagnose the problem.

### Integrity and honesty

Highest priority for Vastpakt.

Every public line must be defensible from:

- user-provided fact;
- local work object;
- real practice case;
- source-near market language;
- bounded public promise.

### Authority

Use real authority only:

- Christophe handles the first example himself;
- practice cases show existing work;
- concrete objects carry credibility.

Do not fake market authority, testimonials, popularity or category leadership.

### Proof of value

Vastpakt should not sell by price comparison. Value is shown by clarity of the work situation and the smallness of the first step.

### Emotion and logic

Safe emotion:

- relief;
- recognition;
- lower uncertainty;
- confidence that someone else can follow the work tomorrow.

Logical support:

- practice cases;
- concrete work objects;
- 24-hour answer;
- fit/no-fit;
- what is sent and what comes back.

### Simplicity

Critical.

Every added object, example or explanation must make the page easier to act on, not heavier to read.

### Specificity

Use specific details only when true:

- Plaud-transcript;
- existing Word-template;
- three mailbox histories;
- Excel output;
- voorraad in kg;
- websitebeheer blocks.

Avoid fake precision.

### Linking

Useful.

Vastpakt should link its offer to things the visitor already knows:

- mail;
- Excel;
- CRM;
- Word;
- Teams;
- offerte;
- factuurmoment;
- afspraak;
- bewijs.

### Harmonize

Important.

Remove or weaken any public sentence that makes the visitor think:

- "that is not my problem";
- "this sounds like an AI agency";
- "this sounds like a project";
- "this sounds like homework";
- "this sounds like another tool."

## Triggers to avoid or use only with hard proof

- greed;
- guilt;
- fake urgency;
- exclusivity;
- desire to belong;
- desire to collect;
- fear pressure;
- curiosity tricks that delay clarity;
- satisfaction guarantees unless there is a real guarantee;
- authority claims without proof.

## Practical grill for next loops

Ask:

1. Does this make the visitor mentally use their own example?
2. Does this resolve a real objection or invent one?
3. Is every line truthful enough to defend in public?
4. Does the line make the first action simpler?
5. Does it harmonize with Flemish KMO workday language?
6. Is specificity factual or decorative?
7. Are we creating trust, or merely using a trigger?

## Current implication for Vastpakt

The homepage already uses several safe triggers:

- involvement: one example;
- credibility: practice cases;
- simplicity: small first step;
- honesty: fit/no-fit;
- authority: Christophe replies himself;
- specificity: concrete work objects.

The next strict risk is objection handling:

The page must resolve likely objections without sounding defensive or written. The strongest next audit should look for places where the visitor may still wonder whether this becomes a bigger project, another tool, or a preparation-heavy intake.
