# Loop 45 - Exa + Swipefile Weak Spots

## Question

Where is the current homepage weakest after loop 44, using Exa market/workday language, local Swipefile discipline, Browser truth, and the Vastpakt copy grill?

## Exa Signals

- myKMO uses ordinary operational language around `handmatig factureren in Excel of Word`, `hangende offertes`, `bevestigde offertes`, `betalingsopvolging`, `waar elk order zich bevindt`, `mailhistoriek`, and `offertes, facturen en herinneringen`.
- Afandi uses market language around `van eerste offerte tot definitieve factuur`, `zonder manuele overschrijving`, `geen dubbele invoer`, `betalingen registreren per document`, and `openstaande saldi`.
- Flemish solo-service pages commonly use contact trust patterns: direct person, answer window, and fit check. But many also use lines like `geen standaardpakket`, `geen verkoopspitch`, and similar anti-sales copy. Those are not safe for Vastpakt after the user's loop 44 rejection.

## Swipefile Discipline

- Keep proof and answer close to the doubt they resolve.
- Lower the form/contact threshold. Do not make the reader feel they need to prepare a package before sending one example.
- Cut writerly anti-sales phrases when a factual line already does the job.

## Browser / Copy Grill Observation

- The public page repeated `voorbeeld` heavily and the main contact path correctly asked for one example.
- Weak spot 1: the offer block said `Wat je meebrengt` and asked for `een paar echte voorbeelden`, which could raise the threshold before contact.
- Weak spot 2: the Christophe block still said `Geen traject om een traject te starten`, which was an invented anti-sales phrase near the user-rejected family of lines.

## Decision

Make a small correction:

1. Change the offer preparation block from a pre-contact ask into a next-step block: `Als Eerste Zicht past`.
2. Remove `Geen traject om een traject te starten` from the person block.

## Classification

- `Als Eerste Zicht past`: light edit / contact-threshold correction using user-confirmed fit/no-fit handling.
- `Dan bekijken we die situatie uit je werkdag`: light edit of the existing one-situation offer.
- `Dan helpen echte voorbeelden...`: Vastpakt translation, but safe because it follows the fit gate and names work objects.
- Removal of `Geen traject om een traject te starten`: user-rejection-adjacent cleanup / anti-copy removal.

## Remaining Risk

- `Eerste Zicht` remains an offer name, not proven market language.
- More use cases should not become public proof until selected and classified.
