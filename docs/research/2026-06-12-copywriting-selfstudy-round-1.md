# Copywriting Selfstudy Round 1: Harry Dry's Three Laws

Date: 2026-06-12

Purpose: self-improvement loop on copywriting craft. Each round studies one source, tests the lessons against existing Vastpakt copy, and upgrades the working checklist if a lesson is fail-able and durable.

Sources this round:

- Harry Dry, Marketing Examples — landing page rewrites with Annie Maguire (marketingexamples.com/landing-page/rewrites)
- Harry Dry on David Perell, "The 3 Rules of Copywriting" and "Learn Copywriting in 76 Minutes" (interview transcripts via search)
- Secondary summary of Dry's 12 writing rules (literaryloop.substack.com)

## Captured lessons (their words)

- `The three laws of copywriting: make it concrete, make it visual, make it falsifiable.`
- `You can't bullshit specifics.`
- `Concrete is [something you can drop on your foot]. If you try and drop "a better way" on your foot, it just fades away.`
- Zoom-in test: write the abstract word at the top of a page, `rewrite it and rewrite it and ask yourself what do I actually mean here ... until I end up with a concrete object.`
- `Never write an ad a competitor can sign.` (Dry quoting Jim Durfee)
- `Customers buy outcomes, not products.`
- `When you've got a unique product the golden rule is to let the product speak for itself.`
- `Write the title only you can.`
- `Take ownership of the real problem.`
- `A great sentence is a good sentence made shorter.` / `a paragraph is ready to ship when there's nothing left to remove.`

## Test against existing Vastpakt copy

Applied the three laws plus the competitor-sign test to the strongest copydeck lines:

| Line | Concrete | Visual | Falsifiable | Competitor can sign? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `Alles loopt. Tot iemand vraagt waar het staat.` | yes | yes (iemand die vraagt) | partly | borderline — een generiek automatiseringsbureau zou dit kunnen tekenen |
| `Als iemand vraagt waar het staat, moet je niet meer zoeken. Je kunt wijzen.` | yes | yes (`wijzen` is droppable-op-je-voet) | yes | nee — dit is de Vastpakt-zin |
| `Je krijgt binnen 24 uur een korte geschreven eerste blik terug, of een eerlijk nee.` | yes | partly | yes (24u, geschreven, of nee) | nee |
| `We maken zichtbaar welke routine vandaag draait op geheugen, losse mails en onduidelijke afspraken.` | yes | yes | partly | borderline |

Finding: the copydeck already passes Dry's laws unusually well — the source-first rules force concreteness. The weakest spot under Dry's lens is **falsifiability outside the 24-uur-belofte**: most lines describe, few lines can be proven wrong. The fix is not new lines but real cases (see werkboek-interview, ronde 1–2: lived proof makes lines falsifiable).

Convergence note: Dry's `you can't bullshit specifics` is the same discipline as our `specific work object > abstract promise`, and his zoom-in test is operationally identical to our `Bronzin -> werkvraag -> zichtbaar maken -> Vastpakt-object` sequence. The new, non-overlapping tools are: the **falsifiable test**, the **competitor-sign test**, and the **drop-it-on-your-foot test** for word choice.

## What Vastpakt must not copy

- Dry's promotional energy (`acing the SAT`, `own it`) without proof behind it — our rules already forbid claimed-not-shown.
- `Customers buy outcomes` as a license for outcome promises: for Vastpakt an outcome line still needs a captured case behind it.
