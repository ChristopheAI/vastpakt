# Loop 71 - Proof To CTA Bridge

## Question

After reading the direct-response manual, where is the next logical homepage weakness?

## Sources checked

- Current rendered/page order in `index.html`.
- Copy classification through loop 70.
- Experiments TSV through loop 70.
- Internal reading note: `2026-05-28-copywriters-padawan-direct-response-reading.md`.

## Direct-response signal

The manual's strongest useful lesson for Vastpakt is not hype or pressure. It is proof discipline:

- one reader;
- one problem;
- one first action;
- proof close to the claim;
- a low-risk next step.

## Verdict

The homepage already has practice proof in the examples grid, but the CTA immediately after it did not explicitly connect those examples to the visitor's own first action.

The risk was subtle: the visitor sees four cases, then gets asked to send one example. The page can make that step easier by saying what the cases have in common: they each start from something concrete.

## Change

Changed the scroller CTA support line from only:

`Neem één mail, offerte, Excel-rij, CRM-notitie of Word-document waar je opnieuw moet zoeken, overtypen of rondvragen.`

to:

`De voorbeelden hierboven gaan telkens over iets concreets: voorraad, websitebeheer, mailboxen of een rapport. Jouw voorbeeld mag even klein zijn: één mail, offerte, Excel-rij, CRM-notitie of Word-document waar je opnieuw moet zoeken, overtypen of rondvragen.`

## Why this is safe

- It points to existing proof objects already on the page.
- It does not add a result claim.
- It does not claim popularity or broad adoption.
- It keeps the first action small.
- It keeps the CTA tied to one submitted example.

## Boundary

Do not turn this into bandwagon/social-proof copy unless there are real numbers, testimonials, or adoption evidence. For Vastpakt, current proof strength comes from concrete practice cases, not from popularity claims.
