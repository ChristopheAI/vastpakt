# Loop 14 - workflow vs werking

Date: 2026-05-27

Purpose: respond to the user clarification that Vastpakt looks at how workflows run inside companies, without turning the public site into generic process/workflow consulting.

## User Clarification

> Vastpakt bekijkt eigenlijk hoe workflows in bedrijven lopen.

## Verdict

Partially correct.

Vastpakt does look at how work moves through a company. But `workflow` should not become the main public problem word.

## Source Signals

Local market-language file:

- `workflows` and `processen` are real market words.
- `proces` is acceptable, but can sound like consultancy.
- `workflow` is understandable to software buyers, cold to many KMO readers.

Local assumption review:

- Do not lead with workflow.
- `werking` should hold more than process flow: status, proof, decision context, and fallback.
- Keep `Eerst een werking zichtbaar maken. Dan pas kiezen wat moet veranderen.`

Local spec:

- Do not make `handwerk`, `workflow`, or `automation` the public-facing problem.
- Readers more often talk about where they stand, what is proven, what may come back later, or which step they cannot forget.

## Decision

Do not put `workflow` in the hero or core CTA.

But make the offer section clearer by explaining the movement:

```text
We kijken mee hoe één stuk werk door mail, Excel, CRM en mensen beweegt: waar het binnenkomt, waar iemand zoekt, wat blijft wachten en wat daarna moet gebeuren.
```

## Classification

- `hoe één stuk werk ... beweegt`: Vastpakt translation of workflow idea into ordinary work language.
- `mail, Excel, CRM en mensen`: market/workday objects.
- `waar het binnenkomt, waar iemand zoekt, wat blijft wachten en wat daarna moet gebeuren`: Vastpakt translation from local work-object fields.

## Remaining Risk

`werk beweegt` is still a Vastpakt phrase. Keep it attached to concrete carriers: mail, Excel, CRM and people.
