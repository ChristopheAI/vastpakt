# Loop 76 - Scannable Proof-To-CTA Bridge

## Question

After reading Web Copy That Sells, where does the homepage route become hardest to scan?

## Sources checked

- `2026-05-28-web-copy-that-sells-reading.md`
- Current scroller CTA after the practice examples.
- Copy classification through loop 75.

## Webcopy signal

Visitors scan first. Headings and first lines should carry the route. One idea per paragraph is safer than one dense paragraph with multiple jobs.

## Verdict

The scroller CTA after the examples had the right content:

- proof objects already shown;
- the visitor's own small example;
- a direct CTA.

But the paragraph did two jobs in one block, making the bridge denser than needed.

## Change

Split the support copy into two short paragraphs:

1. The examples above are concrete: voorraad, websitebeheer, mailboxen or a report.
2. The visitor's example may be just as small: one mail, offer, Excel row, CRM note or Word document.

## Why this is safe

- Same meaning.
- No new claim.
- No new CTA.
- No new proof.
- Better scanability.

## Boundary

Do not turn this into a second offer or a sales-page pitch. It is only a proof-to-action bridge.
