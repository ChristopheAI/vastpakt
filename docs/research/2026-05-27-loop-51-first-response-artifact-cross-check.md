Does a second Exa pass support or weaken the loop 50 idea that Vastpakt needs a small written first-response artifact?

## Finding

The market pattern supports a written first-response artifact, but also shows the trap.

Many adjacent pages promise a fast written output:

- report within 24 hours;
- one-page roadmap;
- PDF blueprint;
- personalized report;
- scorecard;
- priority map;
- written summary;
- next step.

This validates that "we reply" is weaker than "you receive a named thing".

## Trap

Most examples drift too big for Vastpakt:

- AI audit;
- ROI estimates;
- score;
- roadmap;
- blueprint;
- report;
- quick wins;
- implementation plan.

Those formats would push Vastpakt into AI-agency, consulting, or automation-audit territory.

## Strict Verdict

Loop 50 is directionally correct, but the artifact must stay smaller than the market examples.

The safe Vastpakt form is not:

```text
rapport / audit / roadmap / score / plan / quick wins
```

The safer form is:

```text
een kort eerste zicht op jouw voorbeeld
```

It should answer only:

- does this fit;
- where does this one example seem to come loose;
- what question should become visible first;
- what is the smallest next step or next question.

## Copy Boundary

Do not promise:

- full company diagnosis;
- savings estimate;
- ROI;
- priority map for the whole business;
- implementation roadmap;
- tool recommendation;
- automation advice.

## Homepage Implication

If public copy changes, keep the promise close to the submitted example:

```text
op je voorbeeld
```

This phrase prevents overclaiming. It keeps the artifact smaller than competitor audits and clearer than a friendly reply.
