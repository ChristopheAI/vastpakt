# Krits competitor outcome language

Date: 2026-05-31

Status: internal redesign source note. This is not public copy and not approval to copy their wording.

## Why this note exists

Christophe added these Krits pages as another company that can effectively offer the same end outcome:

- `https://krits.be/`
- `https://krits.be/over-krits`

Verdict: partially correct.

Correct part: Krits can compete for the same buyer outcome: less manual work, fewer loose tools, systems that work together, software around the actual process, clearer follow-up and faster execution.

Correction: Krits is not the same first offer as Vastpakt. Krits leads with building maatwerksoftware, AI, webapps, integrations and project delivery. Vastpakt must not become a smaller Krits pitch. Vastpakt's useful distinction is the smaller first step: one concrete work situation made visible before a tool or build decision.

## What is visibly proven

Facts from the public pages:

- Krits positions itself around maatwerksoftware strengthened by AI.
- The homepage opens with software, AI, manual processes and older tools.
- Navigation and solution cards include AI agents/automation, web applications, mobile apps and API integrations.
- The over page explains Krits through a small-team/direct-contact promise, developer access and a no-middle-layer approach.
- Their process explicitly says they first understand the context before writing code.
- Their solutions pages name Excel, manual steps, disconnected bookkeeping/webshop/CRM, double input, quotes, customer follow-up, dashboards and existing tools.
- Their realisations page shows quantified case claims such as less planning work, faster project insight, faster price updates and faster processing.

Claims from Krits:

- They claim to build software around how the company actually works.
- They claim AI makes custom software faster and more affordable.
- They claim the customer talks directly with the developer, not an account manager.
- They claim existing tools can stay and be connected.
- They claim a short first conversation can clarify fit, direction and cost.

Inference:

- Krits is closer to Vastpakt's future-outcome competition than a generic agency because it names the same operational friction: loose tools, Excel/workarounds, manual processes, disconnected systems and context before build.
- Krits is also the clearest risk signal: if Vastpakt uses too much AI/software/build language, the site will sound like a smaller Krits instead of a distinct first-look route.

## Strong mechanisms Vastpakt can borrow

### 1. Friction before offer

Source basis:

- Krits opens the problem layer with manual processes, old tools, too many tools, low result, systems that do not work together and workarounds in Excel.

Why it works:

- The reader recognizes the operational mess before hearing about software.

Vastpakt use:

- The homepage should keep the first recognition layer around today's work:
  - dubbel werk;
  - losse tools/spreadsheets;
  - handmatig overnemen;
  - systemen die niet dezelfde status tonen;
  - offerte/opvolging/contact/CRM/boekhouding niet op één duidelijke plek.

What not to copy:

- Do not jump from recognition to `wij bouwen software`.
- Do not make AI the reason to believe.

### 2. First understand, then build

Source basis:

- Krits says it understands processes and goals before code.

Why it works:

- It makes the technical provider safer because the customer does not need to know the solution first.

Vastpakt use:

- This supports the current Vastpakt route:
  - eerst één concrete werksituatie bekijken;
  - pas daarna bepalen of iets slimmer, duidelijker of automatisch kan.

What not to copy:

- Do not use `analyse` as a heavy consultancy/audit product.
- Do not promise code/build after the first look unless a later project actually exists.

### 3. Direct person, no middle layer

Source basis:

- Krits makes direct developer access part of the trust layer.

Why it works:

- It lowers the fear of a faceless agency process.

Vastpakt use:

- The Christophe block should make directness visible:
  - Christophe leest je situatie zelf;
  - geen accountmanager, geen intake-theater;
  - je krijgt een korte eerste reactie, not a sales route.

What not to copy:

- Do not copy developer/team framing. Vastpakt is person-led, not a software team.

### 4. Existing tools stay

Source basis:

- Krits' integration language says existing tools can remain and be connected.

Why it works:

- This reduces replacement anxiety.

Vastpakt use:

- CTA support and FAQ can say the first step does not require choosing or replacing a tool.

What not to copy:

- Do not imply Vastpakt will connect the tools by default.
- Do not show tool-logo clouds as proof.

### 5. Cases show the outcome category

Source basis:

- Krits cases are outcome-forward: planning, dashboards, data/price sync, admin processing.

Why it works:

- The case surface says what operational change happened, not only what technology was used.

Vastpakt use:

- `/werk/` should classify each case by the work movement:
  - from separate calendars to one planning view;
  - from scattered project data to one dashboard;
  - from manual data/prices to current values;
  - from paper/mail/admin handling to a followable route.

What not to copy:

- Do not borrow their percentage claims or speed claims.
- Do not create quantified Vastpakt case labels without proof.

## Swipefile cards for the redesign

### Card 1: Workarounds in Excel

- Source: over page and solutions pages.
- Observation: Krits names Excel as a sign that the tool does not fit the process.
- Abstract lesson: Excel is a visible workaround, not the enemy.
- Vastpakt use: `/voor-wie/` signal block and homepage project cards.
- Risk: Sounding like an Excel-replacement software vendor.

### Card 2: Direct builder trust

- Source: over page.
- Observation: They remove accountmanager/projectmanager distance as a trust claim.
- Abstract lesson: Direct access can be proof when the work requires context.
- Vastpakt use: Christophe person block and contact expectation.
- Risk: Turning the page into a personal service promise without enough operational proof.

### Card 3: Understand before code

- Source: over page process.
- Observation: The provider earns permission to build by first understanding context.
- Abstract lesson: The first step should be smaller than the final solution.
- Vastpakt use: bridge line and first-look positioning.
- Risk: Making `eerste blik` sound like a full analysis or project proposal.

### Card 4: Same outcome, different route

- Source: homepage, solutions and cases.
- Observation: Krits sells the same desired end state through build and automation.
- Abstract lesson: Vastpakt can name the desired outcome without selling the same route.
- Vastpakt use: outcome hierarchy in the wireframe.
- Risk: Borrowing too much software/AI category language.

### Card 5: One system instead of loose tools

- Source: navigation and webapp/integration pages.
- Observation: The promise compresses scattered tools into one working environment.
- Abstract lesson: The page needs a clear before/after mental model.
- Vastpakt use: `/werk/` old-route/new-route structure.
- Risk: Overpromising a system when Vastpakt may only deliver visibility or a small first response.

## What this changes for the wireframe

Krits should be added beside Flowlift and Let's Develop as an outcome/category competitor.

This does not change the visual blueprint:

- CopyJay remains the homepage spine.
- Pieter remains the personal trust and `/over-mij` blueprint.
- Let's Develop remains a strong operational outcome/use-case source.
- Krits adds the direct warning: do not let Vastpakt become a small AI/software-builder pitch.

Issue `#11` should use Krits to sharpen:

- the `/voor-wie/` signal list;
- the homepage contrast between `nu dubbel werk` and `straks slimmer/duidelijker/automatisch`;
- the Christophe/direct-person trust block;
- the proof boundary on `/werk/`.

## What Vastpakt must not copy

- AI-first positioning.
- Software-builder category.
- `maatwerksoftware` as first public category.
- `bereken uw verlies`, ROI/loss framing or quantified savings.
- Tool-logo clouds.
- Percentage case claims without own proof.
- Team/developer promise if the Vastpakt offer remains Christophe-led.
- `in 30/60 minuten weet u wat het kost` if the first Vastpakt action remains a written first look.

## Open questions

- Is Krits the clearest Flemish competitor for the future build/automation outcome?
- Should `/voor-wie/` explicitly say this is useful before you ask a builder for software?
- Which Vastpakt proof cases can safely show a movement from loose tools to one useful view without implying a full software build?
- How much `AI` should be absent from the first viewport so Vastpakt stays distinct from Krits?

## Decision

Add Krits as a close outcome/category competitor.

Use Krits to sharpen the contrast:

```text
Krits: understands process -> builds AI/software/integrations.
Vastpakt: starts with one stuck work situation -> makes clear what is loose before the tool/build choice.
```
