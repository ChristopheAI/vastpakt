# No B.S. Direct Marketing - Vastpakt reading notes

Date: 2026-05-28
Source: user-provided PDF, `NoB.SDirectMarketingbyDanKennedy.pdf`
Use: internal direct-response discipline for Vastpakt. Not public copy.

## What this source adds

This source is less about clever wording and more about commercial discipline:

1. Every message should have an offer.
2. Every offer should have a clear next action.
3. The next action should match the visitor's threshold.
4. Follow-up matters.
5. Results and response matter more than taste.
6. The message must fit the market and the medium.
7. Generic communication is weak because it speaks to nobody in particular.

## Vastpakt translation

### Offer

Vastpakt's first offer is not a full project, audit, roadmap, automation package, or software build.

The first offer is:

`Stuur één voorbeeld. Christophe antwoordt binnen 24 uur wat opvalt, of dit past, en welke vraag eerst zichtbaar moet worden.`

This must stay small, concrete, and tied to the submitted example.

### Threshold resistance

The PDF's useful concept is threshold resistance: some offers feel too scary or too big for first contact.

Vastpakt implication:

- `Stuur één voorbeeld` is the low-threshold action.
- `Eerste Zicht` is the small middle offer.
- Any language that sounds like audit, roadmap, workshop, report, project, implementation, or process transformation raises the threshold too early.

### Clear instructions

The PDF repeatedly says people need to be told exactly what to do next.

Vastpakt implication:

- Keep CTA language direct.
- Keep explaining what to send: one mail, offerte, Excel-rij, CRM-notitie, Word-document or afspraak.
- Keep explaining what comes back: fit/no-fit, what is loose, what first needs to become visible.

### Follow-up

The PDF treats follow-up as a core rule, not an afterthought.

Vastpakt implication:

- The 24-hour reply promise is commercially important.
- It should not be buried.
- It should not be repeated everywhere either; keep it near person/contact where action doubt is highest.

### No paid brand-building

The source attacks vague branding and image advertising.

Vastpakt implication:

- Avoid making the homepage a brand mood piece.
- Avoid LinkedIn-style slogans as homepage substance.
- Every section needs a job: recognition, proof, offer clarity, fit boundary, trust, contact.

### Strong copy without noise

The PDF says strong copy does not mean shouting louder.

Vastpakt implication:

- Stay direct but sober.
- Strong for Vastpakt means specific work objects and visible before/after, not aggressive urgency.
- Do not import hype, fake scarcity, FOMO, or discount logic.

### Message-market-media

The source's message-market-media triangle maps well to Vastpakt.

Vastpakt version:

- Market: Flemish KMO operators with manual work, loose tools, unclear follow-up.
- Message: first make one concrete work situation visible.
- Medium: homepage must lead to one low-threshold example submission, not a broad brand statement.

### Ideal customer

The PDF is strict about not speaking to everybody.

Vastpakt implication:

- Do not drift toward everyone with "tools, data, processes, AI".
- Stay with the operator who recognizes mail, Excel, CRM, Word, facturatie, afspraak, bewijs, status and next step.

## What Vastpakt should not import

- Fake urgency.
- Fear-based pressure.
- Aggressive direct-response tone.
- Discount/bonus logic.
- Big money claims.
- Broad brand attacks.
- Popularity claims without evidence.
- Copying direct-marketing layouts or mail-order visual style.

## Practical grill for next homepage loops

Ask:

1. What offer does this section make?
2. Is the next action obvious?
3. Is the threshold too high?
4. Does this section create response or only brand mood?
5. Is the message written for one real market, or for anyone?
6. Does the proof make the action easier?
7. What follow-up expectation does the visitor have after this line?

## Current implication for Vastpakt

The homepage is moving in the right direction because it already has a low-threshold CTA and a clear 24-hour contact promise.

The next strict risk is offer clarity:

- `Stuur één voorbeeld` is clear.
- `Eerste Zicht` is still an offer name that must be supported by plain explanation.
- The page should keep making the first action feel smaller than a project, but more concrete than a contact form.
