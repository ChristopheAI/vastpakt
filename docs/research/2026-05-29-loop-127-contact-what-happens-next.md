# Loop 127 - Contact "What Happens Next", Hero Proofline Hold

Date: 2026-05-29

## Decision Intent

Check the hero proofline and the contact intro against the confirmed first-response artifact (DESIGN.md section 8). Align where there is a real gap, hold where alignment would force.

## Two Targets, Two Verdicts

### Contact intro: real gap, aligned

The contact section asked for input (describe where it stays stuck, no file needed) but never said what comes back. The decisive moment, the submit button, carried no offer certainty.

Exa was unanimous:

- Vladimir Siedykh (`https://vladimirsiedykh.com/blog/contact-page-design-qualified-inquiries-2025`): "People hesitate when they do not know what happens after they submit. A short sentence about what they will receive, and when, removes that anxiety."
- Specflux (`https://www.specflux.com/web-design/ux-writing-for-conversions-2/`): next-step clarity near the CTA, "We'll send a personalized report within 2 hours" sets the timeline and removes uncertainty.
- Verlua (`https://www.verlua.com/blog/contact-form-optimization-guide`): a specific response-time promise "removes the fear that the inquiry will disappear into a void."
- Aether (`https://aether-agency.co.uk/insights/microcopy-ux-writing`) and Express Jam (`https://www.expressjs.org/how-to-design-a-contact-page-that-actually-gets-submissions/`): reassurance right at the submit moment, "we will get back to you within 24 hours."

Change: added one line directly under the submit button, pulled from section 8, using the same words as the offer section (verbal match):

```text
Je krijgt binnen 24 uur een korte geschreven eerste blik terug, of een eerlijk nee.
```

Verdict: keep. Browser-verified under the button, no overflow, muted. It echoes the artifact without repeating the full four-part structure. Sharper, not heavier.

### Hero proofline: already consistent, hold

```text
Eerst één concrete plek zichtbaar maken. Dan pas kiezen wat moet veranderen.
```

This is the method principle (make one place visible first, decide later), not the deliverable. It does not contradict section 8. Forcing the artifact ("geschreven eerste blik") into the hero would pull the concrete deliverable above the fold, before the offer section explains it, which is premature and heavier, not sharper. The hero already carries enough certainty ("Stuur één voorbeeld" plus "Geen nieuwe tool als eerste antwoord").

Verdict: no change. Alignment without a real gap would add weight. Held on purpose.
