# Loop 46 - Offer Name Load Review

## Question

After loop 45, is the next weak spot copy, proof, or the way the offer name carries too much of the lower page?

## Exa Signals

- Rezolv-style scan pages use strong offer labels like review/scan and then explain them through maturity, roadmap, quick wins, and recommendations.
- Codelines-style KMO process pages are closer to Vastpakt when they stay with `waar info vandaag zit`, `één proces`, `Excel`, `e-mail`, `losse systemen`, `minder kopiëren`, and `minder even checken`.
- Office Consulting-style backoffice pages use recognizable situations such as handmatige Word/Excel facturen, unclear taakverdeling/opvolging, documents in mail/laptop/cloud, and missing overview.

## Swipefile Discipline

- A named offer can help orientation, but it should not be forced into every reassurance line.
- At contact and qualification moments, the reader needs plain fit language more than a repeated branded label.
- Keep the offer name as a section marker; use ordinary language for the decision.

## Browser / Copy Grill Observation

- `Eerste Zicht` appeared repeatedly in offer, method, qualification, person, and contact support copy.
- `zinvol` also repeated across the lower page.
- This made the lower page lean on an unproven offer name instead of the simpler question: does this fit this example?

## Decision

Keep `Eerste Zicht` as the offer name where it orients the page.

Reduce it in lower fit/response lines:

- `of Eerste Zicht zinvol is` -> `of dit past`
- `Als Eerste Zicht past` -> `Als dit past`
- `Eerste Zicht zinvol is` -> `dit past`
- `Vastpakt zinvol is` -> `dit past`

## Classification

- `dit past`: light edit / plain fit language based on user-confirmed honest fit/no-fit handling.
- No new public claim added.
- No proof claim changed.

## Remaining Risk

- `Eerste Zicht` is still an offer name needing market reaction, but it now carries less of the lower-page decision logic.
